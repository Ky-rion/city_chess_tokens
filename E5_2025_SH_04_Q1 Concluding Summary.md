### 2. The Semi-Empirical Mass Formula (Fission)

Used for **large nuclei** (like Uranium) where the nucleus behaves like a liquid drop.

The Formula (Binding Energy $B$):

$$B(A, Z) = a_v A - a_s A^{2/3} - a_c \frac{Z(Z-1)}{A^{1/3}} - a_{sym} \frac{(A-2Z)^2}{A}$$

**The Coefficients:**

-   **Volume ($a_v$):** $15.5 \text{ MeV}$
    
-   **Surface ($a_s$):** $16.8 \text{ MeV}$
    
-   **Coulomb ($a_c$):** $0.72 \text{ MeV}$
    
-   **Symmetry ($a_{sym}$):** $23.0 \text{ MeV}$
    

Reaction Energy ($Q$):

$$Q = \sum B_{products} - \sum B_{reactants}$$

---

### 3. Exact Mass Calculation (Fusion)

Used for **small nuclei** (like Hydrogen/Helium) where the liquid drop model fails.

The Formula (Mass Defect):

$$Q = [ \text{Mass}_{reactants} - \text{Mass}_{products} ] \times c^2$$

**Calculation Step:**

1.  Sum the atomic masses of the start ($M_{start}$).
    
2.  Sum the atomic masses of the end ($M_{end}$).
    
3.  Find difference $\Delta m$ in atomic mass units ($u$).
    
4.  Convert to Energy: $E = \Delta m \times 931.5 \text{ MeV}$.
    

---

### 4. Scaling to the Real World (Per Gram)

How to turn atomic energy into real-world power.

Step 1: Find Atoms per Gram

$$N = \frac{1 \text{ g}}{\text{Molar Mass (g/mol)}} \times N_A$$

Step 2: Total Energy

$$E_{total} = N \times Q_{\text{per reaction}}$$

**Comparison Fact:**

-   **Nuclear Fission Density:** $\sim 1 \text{ g Uranium} \approx 2.2 \text{ metric tons of Coal}$
    
-   **Nuclear Fusion Density:** $\sim 1 \text{ g Fusion Fuel} \approx 4 \times \text{Fission Energy}$
