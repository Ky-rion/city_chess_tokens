Here is the explanation for why these specific neutrinos are "mono-energetic" (meaning they all have exactly the same energy), unlike most other neutrinos.

---

### 1. The Setup: "Two-Body" vs. "Three-Body"

To understand this, we have to look at the "Before" and "After" of the reaction.

The Reaction:

$$e^- + {}^7\text{Be} \rightarrow {}^7\text{Li} + \nu_e$$

-   **Before (Initial State):** We have a Beryllium-7 nucleus and an electron (from the inner shell of the atom) essentially sitting together at rest.
    
-   **After (Final State):** We have **two** particles flying away: a Lithium-7 nucleus and a neutrino ($\nu_e$).
    

This is what physicists call a **Two-Body Final State**. This is the key to the whole answer.

### 2. The Conservation Laws

Nature has strict accounting rules. Two of the most important are:

1.  Conservation of Energy: The total energy before must equal the total energy after. This energy comes from the difference in mass between the reactants and the products (the $Q$-value).
    
    $$E_{\text{available}} = E_{\text{Li}} + E_{\nu}$$
    
2.  Conservation of Momentum: The total momentum must stay zero (since the Beryllium nucleus was effectively sitting still).
    
    $$p_{\text{Li}} + p_{\nu} = 0 \implies p_{\text{Li}} = -p_{\nu}$$
    

This means the Lithium nucleus and the neutrino **must** fly off in exactly opposite directions with equal (but opposite) momenta.

### 3. The Constraint (Why it is fixed)

Because there are only **two** objects in the final state, the physics is "rigid" or fully constrained.

Imagine two ice skaters of different sizes (a big one named Lithium and a tiny one named Neutrino) standing together at rest. They push off each other.

-   Because of Newton's laws (momentum), there is only **one specific speed** the tiny skater can possibly go. They can't choose to go slower or faster arbitrarily; the physics is locked by their masses.
    

Since the mass of the Lithium nucleus is fixed (the problem tells us to assume it's in the ground state) and the mass of the neutrino is fixed (effectively zero), there is only **one mathematical solution** for how the energy is shared.

The neutrino gets a specific, fixed slice of the energy pie every single time. Thus: **Mono-energetic.**

### 4. Comparison: Why is this special?

To really appreciate this, you have to compare it to standard **Beta Decay** (which is what usually happens in reactors or other parts of the sun).

In standard Beta Decay ($n \rightarrow p + e^- + \bar{\nu}_e$), the final state has **three** particles:

1.  A proton
    
2.  An electron
    
3.  A neutrino
    

This is a "Three-Body Final State." Using the ice skater analogy: imagine three skaters pushing off each other.

Now, the energy sharing is flexible! The electron could take a lot of energy, leaving the neutrino with a little. Or the neutrino could take a lot, leaving the electron with a little. This results in a continuous spectrum of energies, not a single sharp peak.

### Summary

The neutrino is mono-energetic because the reaction results in a two-body final state.

Because momentum and energy must both be conserved, the energy is split between the Lithium nucleus and the neutrino in a fixed, unique ratio. There is no third particle to "steal" variable amounts of energy.
